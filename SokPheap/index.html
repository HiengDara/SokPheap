Our idea introduces a sophisticated health monitoring app tailored for Cambodia, targeting the high incidence of noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) like heart disease and diabetes. Leveraging advanced AI algorithms, the app analyzes data from wearable devices or manual entries to provide personalized health insights and recommendations, further to personalized natural curing. About Us

Your Health, Our Mission

At SokPheap, we transform health management through a seamless process: collect user data via manual input or wearable sync, analyze risks with AI, provide personalized advice, track progress with gamified feedback, adapt recommendations dynamically, and ensure regular check-ups with preventive remindersâ€”all to help you achieve optimal health and prevent NCDs.

Data Collection

Utilize connected wearable devices to track vital signs and other health data. Include manual data entry options for users without wearable devices.

AI Health Buddy

deploy AI algorithms to analyze health data, predict health risks, and provide actionable health advice.

User Engagement

Implement features for symptom recognition, lifestyle modification recommendations, and proactive health management.

Technology Used

We use Flutter for the app, Google Colab and Kaggle for data analytics, structured and unstructured health data, AI for predictive analytics, and IoT with cloud computing for data aggregation and analysis.

Our Three Main Objectives

Predict, Prevent, Protect - Empowering Health with AI

Promote Early Detection

Use AI-driven analytics to identify early signs of chronic diseases, enabling timely intervention.AI analyzes data to identify potential health issues early.

Encourage Healthy Lifestyles

Offer personalized advice and tools that motivate users to adopt healthier habits, such as better diet and increased physical activity.

Reduce Disease Impact

Help lessen the severity and progression of chronic diseases through regular health monitoring and preventive care, ultimately aiming to decrease the incidence of NCDs and healthcare costs.
